DELTA Course

Course Information

The DELTA features a balance of theory and practice to enhance your understanding of the English language, while examining in depth the principles of effective teaching and the practical skills required for teaching English in different contexts.

There are 3 DELTA modules which can be taken separately

Courses Start - End date Available seats Fee  
DELTA 1 22 Feb - 15 Apr 2016 12 $ 3,995 Apply now
Module 1 : A 3 hour written examination. Preparation for this can be done on a part-time basis.
Module 2 : An intensive 8 week course which includes teaching practice and assessment and a range of practically focused, in-depth written assignments.
Module 3 : A written assignment on course and syllabus design which is completed on a part-time basis.

These three modules are flexible so you can:

Take all three modules at one time, two together or just one.

Receive a certificate and credit for each module you successfully complete.

Take modules when you choose over any period of time (there is no time limit to complete all three modules).

Modules description

Modules 1 & 3

Both of these modules can be completed on a part-time basis at any time, and during our Module 2 course you can choose to attend input sessions that focus on these modules.

You can take the Module 1 exam and submit your Module 3 assignment at our centre in early June or December.

For more assistance and information about these (incl. submission dates), please contact delta@ilavietnam.com

Module 2

We run an intensive Module 2 course which is conducted on a full-time basis over 8 weeks in Ho Chi Minh City. It includes:

Input aimed at combining theory with practice

Teaching practice and assessment.

Peer observations

A range of practically focused written assignments

Optional input on Modules 1 and 3

Course Hours

The course usually runs from Monday to Friday from 8.45am - 5.30pm.

There are 120 contact hours with fellow candidates and course tutors with over 10 hours of teaching adults and 10 hours observing your peers.

Outside of these hours, you can expect to spend over 300 hours on reading, research and assignment writing.

It is not possible to work whilst attending the Module 2 intensive course

Teaching Practice

During the course you'll practise teaching with at least two different levels of adult learners.

You will have 4 assessed lessons which will be evenly distributed throughout the 8 week period.

The course also includes a fair amount of unassessed teaching practice, which is incredibly useful as it allows you to experiment with your teaching, further understand the learners and gain valuable feedback on your progress.
